Funder summary (agent-generated)

NSF's TechAccess program (NSF 26-508) establishes regional Coordination Hubs that broaden equitable access to AI literacy and trustworthy AI use across communities historically underrepresented in computing. Hubs convene libraries, makerspaces, community colleges, and grassroots educators to deliver place-based AI-readiness programming, with explicit emphasis on workforce relevance and informal learning settings. Awards range $1.5M–$3M over 36 months, with cost-share encouraged but not required.

Drafted Letter of Intent — preview

1 · Project Vision

Hacker Dojo proposes to anchor the Silicon Valley AI-Ready Hub, a regional coordination node convening makerspaces, public libraries, community colleges, and grassroots educators across San Mateo, Santa Clara, and Alameda counties. Our shared aim is to ensure that residents historically underrepresented in computing — including women, first-generation learners, returning workers, and non-native English speakers — gain foundational AI literacy and concrete tools to apply AI to their own work and lives.

2 · Why Hacker Dojo

For 16 years, Hacker Dojo has operated as the Bay Area's largest community-run makerspace, serving over 4,200 active members and hosting 380+ public events annually. We are not a credentialed training institution — and that is precisely the point. NSF's call recognizes that AI readiness must reach people where they already gather to learn informally. Our 2025 Impact Report documents 3,100 unique program participants, 41% identifying as first-generation, and an 84% completion rate across our workshop sequences.

3 · Proposed Hub Activities

The Hub will deliver three coordinated programs: (a) a 10-site AI-Ready Educator Fellowship training informal educators across the region; (b) a community curriculum library co-developed with partner sites and openly licensed; (c) a quarterly regional summit and ongoing community of practice.

4 · Partners & Cost Share

Confirmed partners include Mountain View Public Library, Foothill–De Anza Community College District, and Code2College, with additional letters of partnership in progress. Hacker Dojo will contribute $180,000 in matched space, staff time, and tooling over the project period.
