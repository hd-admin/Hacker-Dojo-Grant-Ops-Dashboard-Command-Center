## Project Title

Expanding Community STEM Learning Labs at Hacker Dojo

## Executive Summary

Hacker Dojo respectfully requests $50,000 from Infosys Foundation USA through the Community STEM Learning Labs Grant to expand access to hands-on STEM learning in Silicon Valley. Hacker Dojo's mission is community innovation and education, and its programs are designed to open doors for learners, makers, families, and community members who want practical experience with technology.

This funding will support a community STEM learning lab model that combines workshops, makerspace training, youth STEM nights, and community-centered innovation programming. Through this project, Hacker Dojo will strengthen its ability to deliver accessible, hands-on learning experiences that help participants build confidence, technical skills, and a sense of belonging in STEM.

Grounded in Hacker Dojo's existing program model, the proposed project will expand the reach and consistency of technology education activities already central to the organization: workshops, makerspace training, youth STEM nights, and nonprofit startup support. The grant will help fund equipment, instructional materials, program delivery, volunteer and instructor support, and outreach to ensure more community members can participate.

## Organization Overview

Hacker Dojo is a nonprofit organization focused on community innovation and education. Hacker Dojo expands access to technology education and community innovation in Silicon Valley through programs that include workshops, makerspace training, youth STEM nights, and nonprofit startup support.

Hacker Dojo's approach is rooted in the belief that technology learning should be practical, collaborative, and open to the broader community. By providing shared learning space, hands-on activities, and peer connection, Hacker Dojo helps participants move from curiosity to capability.

## Statement of Need

Silicon Valley is a global center of technology, yet access to hands-on STEM learning opportunities is not evenly distributed. Many learners and families benefit from interest in science, technology, engineering, and math, but still face barriers to meaningful participation in community-based learning environments that offer tools, instruction, mentorship, and room to experiment.

Community STEM learning labs help close that gap. They provide more than one-time exposure to STEM topics; they create ongoing opportunities for participants to build, test, troubleshoot, and learn with others. This kind of environment is especially valuable for youth, beginners, and community members who may not otherwise see themselves reflected in traditional technology pathways.

Hacker Dojo is well positioned to address this need because its current programming already connects education with community innovation. With additional support, Hacker Dojo can expand the capacity, consistency, and accessibility of these experiences so that more participants can engage in hands-on STEM learning in a welcoming community setting.

## Project Description

With support from Infosys Foundation USA, Hacker Dojo will expand its Community STEM Learning Labs programming through four connected activity areas:

### 1. Hands-On STEM Workshops

Hacker Dojo will offer workshops that introduce participants to practical technology skills through project-based learning. Topics may include coding, electronics, digital fabrication, design thinking, and maker-centered problem solving. Workshops will emphasize active participation and real-world application rather than passive instruction.

### 2. Makerspace Training

The project will strengthen makerspace training so that participants can safely and confidently use shared tools, equipment, and collaborative work areas. Training will help learners build technical fluency while also developing habits of experimentation, responsibility, and peer learning.

### 3. Youth STEM Nights

Hacker Dojo will continue and expand youth STEM nights that welcome young people and families into an engaging, community-based STEM environment. These events will expose participants to interactive projects and make STEM more approachable, creative, and social.

### 4. Community Innovation and Nonprofit Support

Because Hacker Dojo's model connects education with broader community innovation, the project will also support community-serving innovators and nonprofit startup activity where appropriate. This helps reinforce the idea that STEM learning can lead to real community problem-solving, not just technical skill development in isolation.

## Project Goals and Outcomes

The primary goal of this project is to expand equitable access to hands-on STEM learning opportunities through a community learning lab model.

Expected outcomes include:

- Increased access to workshops, makerspace learning, and youth STEM experiences.
- Increased participant confidence and familiarity with STEM tools, concepts, and practices.
- Stronger community engagement around technology education and innovation.
- Greater capacity for Hacker Dojo to sustain and grow community-based STEM learning opportunities.

### Illustrative Performance Measures

The following measures can be tailored to the final application format:

- Number of workshops delivered during the grant period.
- Number of participants engaged across workshops, makerspace training, and youth STEM nights.
- Number of youth and families participating in STEM engagement events.
- Participant feedback indicating increased confidence, interest, or skill development.
- Number of instructional sessions or lab activities supported by new equipment and materials.

## Evaluation Plan

Hacker Dojo will evaluate the project using practical, program-level measures aligned with community STEM engagement goals. Evaluation activities may include:

- Attendance and participation tracking.
- Workshop and event completion records.
- Participant feedback surveys.
- Facilitator observations of engagement and skill-building.
