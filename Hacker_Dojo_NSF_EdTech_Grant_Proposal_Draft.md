## 2. Project Summary

### Overview

Hacker Dojo proposes the **Tech Access Expansion Initiative**, a comprehensive program to democratize technology education and community innovation in Silicon Valley. By leveraging our established infrastructure, proven workshop curricula, and deep community ties, we will scale our impact to serve 2,500 additional learners annually—prioritizing underserved youth, first-generation college students, and community members facing barriers to technology careers.

### Intellectual Merit

This project advances evidence-based EdTech practices by integrating hands-on makerspace training with structured STEM education pathways. Our model treats community spaces as living laboratories where learners prototype real solutions to real problems—building both technical competence and collaborative problem-solving skills that employers increasingly demand.

### Broader Impacts

- **Geographic Reach:** Serving the Silicon Valley region, with emphasis on East San Jose, a community where technology career pathways remain inaccessible to most residents despite proximity to industry.
- **Demographic Reach:** Targeting populations historically underrepresented in technology: 60% of participants from low-income households, 55% from communities of color, 40% first-generation technology learners.
- **Economic Mobility:** Building pathways to living-wage technology careers and supporting nonprofit startups that address community-identified needs.
- **Knowledge Sharing:** Open-sourcing curricula, publishing outcomes data, and providing a replicable model for community-based technology education nationwide.

## 3. Project Description

### 3.1 Statement of Need

Silicon Valley sits amid stark educational inequality. While technology companies generate billions in value, most community members—especially those from East San Jose and surrounding lower-income areas—remain excluded from the skills, networks, and opportunities the tech economy offers. The median household income in East San Jose is $71,000 below the Santa Clara County average. Unemployment rates in these neighborhoods consistently run 2-3x the county average.

The need is not for theoretical technology education. The need is for:

1. **Access to equipment and spaces** — 3D printers, laser cutters, electronics workbenches, and collaborative workspaces that cost tens of thousands of dollars to establish.
2. **Mentorship from practitioners** — Exposure to people actually working in technology roles who can demystify career pathways.
3. **Project-based learning that counts** — Credentials, portfolios, and demonstrated competencies that translate to jobs or further education.
4. **Community belonging** — A sense of being welcomed into technology culture rather than being treated as an outsider.

Hacker Dojo has spent years proving that these needs, when addressed together, produce transformative outcomes. We have documented learners who entered as first-time coders and now work as software engineers, developers, and makers who have launched products used by thousands.

### 3.2 Theory of Change

```
IF Hacker Dojo provides:
  - Free/sliding-scale access to makerspace equipment
  - Structured workshops in high-demand technology skills
  - Youth STEM programming with family engagement
  - Mentorship from working technology professionals
  - Pathways from beginner to portfolio-ready practitioner

THEN participants will:
  - Acquire employable technology skills
  - Build professional networks in the tech industry
  - Launch projects and businesses that serve their communities
  - Pursue further education in STEM fields

BECAUSE: Hands-on, community-embedded learning with mentorship
         produces better outcomes than traditional classroom instruction
         for adult learners and youth from underrepresented backgrounds.
```

### 3.3 Program Components

#### 3.3.1 Core Workshop Series

**Adult Technology Bootcamps (500 participants/year)**
- 8-week intensive programs in:
  - Web development (full-stack JavaScript)
  - Data analytics and visualization
  - Electronics and IoT prototyping
  - Digital fabrication and manufacturing
- Curriculum developed in partnership with local employers to ensure job-relevant skills
- Weekly evening schedule (6pm-9pm) accommodating working adults
- Stipends available for participants who complete 90%+ attendance

**Youth STEM Nights (1,200 participants/year)**
- Weekly drop-in sessions for K-12 students
- Themed projects: robotics, app development, game design, digital art
- Family participation requirement for ages 8-12, building home learning environments
- Connection to school-day STEM programs for reinforcement

#### 3.3.2 Makerspace Training Program

**Equipment Access and Certification (800 participants/year)**
- Multi-session certification courses for:
  - 3D printing and modeling
  - Laser cutting and etching
  - Electronics assembly and testing
  - CNC machining
- Safety certification required before independent equipment use
- Project storage and team collaboration spaces
- Open lab hours (40+ hours/week) for certified users

#### 3.3.3 Nonprofit Startup Support

**Community Innovation Lab (scalable: 20-40 startups/year)**
- 6-month accelerator program for community-serving nonprofits
- Technical mentorship and pro-bono development services
- Workspace and equipment access
- Focus areas: education technology, community health, environmental justice, economic development

## 8. Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources

### Facility Description

Hacker Dojo operates a 4,500 square foot facility in Mountain View, California, centrally located in the Silicon Valley corridor and accessible via public transit. The space includes:

- **Open Workshop Floor (2,500 sq ft):** Flexible makerspace with 15 workbenches, storage for 50 active projects, and collaborative seating areas
- **Electronics Lab (500 sq ft):** 10 dedicated electronics workbenches with oscilloscopes, soldering stations, and component inventory
- **Fabrication Room (600 sq ft):** Houses 3D printers, laser cutter, CNC mill, and paint booth
- **Classroom (400 sq ft):** 30-seat instructional space with smart board and workstation for each student
- **Community Lounge (500 sq ft):** Networking events, informal collaboration, and startup team workspace

### Equipment Inventory

Hacker Dojo maintains over $150,000 in current equipment assets, including:

- 8 3D printers (consumer and professional grade)
- 1 industrial laser cutter
- 2 CNC mills (desktop and professional)
- Electronics test and assembly equipment
- 25 computers for instructional use
- Audio/video production equipment

### Leverage and Partnerships

- **Google.org:** In-kind technical mentorship program
